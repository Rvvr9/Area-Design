Level Designer • Area Designer • Developer

I am interested in building spaces that guide attention, shape movement, and create atmosphere through layout, framing, scale, and visual rhythm. This page presents a selection of level and area design work in a more visual, artbook-style format.

Level Design Portfolio

The first step in my level design process is writing out a design document or sketchign a rough layout, that conveys what the player should be getting from this level. I like to think about the emotional beats, pacing, and visual flow of a space before I start building.

Aother important step in my process is simply writing out everything that is solidfied in the area that I will be desiging. This document is a reference to the vertical slice area and what the player is able to do in this area / stage of the game. This is extremely important for level and area design, because now I know what the player can do, and I can design around those mechanics and interactions.

This document is all about turning our creative visions into actionable plans and solid designs that actually fit in the game. Taking multiple ideas, from yourself and or your team, and distilling them into a cohesive vision is one of the most important parts of the design process.

This is a close up document of turning a concept into an actual design. I start with simple mood boards to get everyone on the same page, from there I build designs and layouts that I can point to and say "this is the kind of space I want to build". This is a crucial step in the design process, because it allows you to get feedback on your ideas before you start building, and it also allows you to communicate your vision to your team in a clear and concise way.
